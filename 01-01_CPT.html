PBMCs Isolation From CPT™ Tube

This article provides details on the chemical analysis experiment and includes information on the chemicals used.

Materials Consumables Instruments & Equipments
1. FBS Sigma (Cat. #F2442 , Lot number #22A433) 1. BD vacutainer Cell Preparation Tubes with sodium heparin (Cat. no.362753) 1. Sorvall LYNX-6000 Superspeed centrifuge
2. Phosphate-Buffered Saline (PBS, GIBCO Cat. #10010049) 2. 50 mL centrifuge tube 2. Eppendorf 5810R centrifuge
3. ACK Lysing Buffer (Thermo, A10492) 3. Serological pipette 3. Pipette gun/PipetteX

Preparation of Reagents and Media

Freshly prepare (~80 mL/sample)

Wash buffer (1% FBS, 1 mM EDTA)
Store at 4°C up to 1 week
Reagents Volume
- PBS, pH 7.4 [Gibco, #10010049] 500 mL
- FBS [Sigma] 5 mL
- UltraPure 0.5 M EDTA, pH8.0 [Invitrogen, #15575020] 1 mL

PBMCs Isolation

  1. Ensure the CPT Tube is at room temperature (RT; 18-25°C) and properly labeled for patient identification.

  2. After blood collection, store the tube upright at RT until centrifugation. For optimal results, blood samples should ideally be centrifuged within 2 hrs of blood collection.

  3. Centrifuge the CPT tube with blood sample using 5920R Eppendorf Centrifuge at 20°C with a horizontal rotor (swing-out head) for 30 mins at 1500 RCF (Relative Centrifugal Force). (Speed change of accel/decel: Soft, 7/7)
    NOTE: Remix the blood sample immediately prior to centrifugation by gently inverting the tube 8 to 10 times.

  4. Following centrifugation, a white layer of mononuclear cells and platelets will form slightly under the plasma layer. Aspirate approximately half of the plasma without disturbing the cell layer. Collect cell layer by pouring it into a 50 mL conical centrifuge tube with a cap. Collecting cells immediately after centrifugation will yield best results.

  5. Spin down the collected mixture using a centrifuge for 15 mins at 300 RCF (Soft, 7/7) at 20°C. Aspirate as much supernatant as possible without disturbing the cell pellet.
    NOTE: Use a pasteur pipette to the remove supernatant.

  6. Resuspend the cell pellet with 3 mL of ACK lysing buffer using a 5-mL pipette and place it at RT for 3 mins

  7. 1st wash: Add Wash buffer to bring the volume to 50 mL. Cap the tube, mix cells by inverting the tube 5 times and avoid making bubbles during pipetting.

  8. Centrifuge for 15 mins at 300 RCF at 20°C (Soft, 7/7). Aspirate as much supernatant as possible without disturbing the cell pellet.

  9. Resuspend the cell pellet by tapping the tube with the index finger.

  10. 2nd wash: Add Wash buffer to bring the volume to 20 mL. Cap the tube and mix cells by inverting the tube 5 times.

  11. Centrifuge for 15 mins at 300 RCF at 20°C (Soft, 7/7). Aspirate as much supernatant as possible without disturbing the cell pellet.

  12. Resuspend the cell pellet in 2-3 mL of Wash buffer and aliquot 10-15 μL for cell counting (Concentration tends to range from 5x104 - 1x107 cells/mL).

  13. Centrifuge the remaining suspension for 15 mins at 300 RCF (Soft, 7/7) at 20°C. Aspirate as much supernatant as possible without disturbing the cell pellet.
    NOTE: Immediately centrifuge after taking cells for counting. After centrifugation, discard the supernatant, then add CryoStor. If the counting step takes too long, remove supernatant and place cell on ice before adding CryoStor according to the calculation.

  14. Resuspend the cell pellet in 2-3 mL of CryoStor and aliquot into 2-4 cryotubes.
    NOTE: If collected cells > 9 M/mL, add 3 mL of CryoStor. | Fixed volume - 0.5 mL 2 cryotubes and 1mL 2 cryotubes.
          If collected cells < 9 M/mL, calculate and Resuspend the cell pellet in CryoStor to 3x106 cells/mL | Fixed number of cells - 1.5M 2 cryotubes, 3M 1 cryotube and leftover 1 cryotube.

Cell Counting

Collected PBMC viability > 90%

  1. Resuspend the cell pellet from step 11 (after 2nd wash) in Wash buffer to bring the concentration to 1×106 cells/mL. (Step 12)

  2. Take 15 μL of the cell suspension into 0.2 mL centrifuge tube, add 15 μL of Trypan Blue (ratio 1:1) and mix it for cell counting.

  3. Take 10 μL of stained cells into countess slide and put it in countess machine.
    NOTE: Put countess slide into countess machine 2 times. Using concentration of live cells from 2nd time for calculation.

  4. Centrifuge the remaining suspension for 15 mins at 300 RCF. Aspirate as much supernatant as possible without disturbing the cell pellet. (Step 13)

  5. Resuspend the cells into 1 mL of CryoStor in a cryotube for freezing. (Step 14)
