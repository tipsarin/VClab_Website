Cell preparation: Thawing cells (Demuxlet protocol)

Thawing and Preparation

  1. Add 9 mL of thawing media at 37°C into a 15 mL Falcon tube. (Prepare 16 tubes)

    2. When ready!!

  2. Transfer 16 tubes of PBMCs to a 37°C water bath and thaw for 1-2 mins (depends on volume in cryotube). (Thaw 1-2 tubes at a time)

  3. After thawing cells, open the cryotube under the laminar hood and add 1 mL of warmed thawing media dropwise, using wide-bore blue tips.

  4. Gently transfer the entire cell suspension (cells with 1 mL thawing media from step 3) into the 15 mL Falcon tube containing 9 mL of warmed thawing media wide-bore blue tips.
    NOTE: Add the suspension gently, either dropwise or while keeping the tube at a 45° angle.

  5. Mix the solution extremely gently by pipetting (3-4 times) using wide-bore blue tips, avoiding any bubbling.
    NOTE: Cells at this stage are very fragile!

  6. Centrifuge at 300 g for 5 mins and discard the supernatant.

  7. 1st wash: Resuspend the cell pellet in 5 mL of warmed thawing media by pipetting (5-10 times) gently using wide-bore blue tips.

  8. Centrifuge at 300 g for 5 mins and discard the supernatant.

  9. 2nd wash: Resuspend the cell pellet in 3 mL of PBS + 0.04% BSA.

  10. Centrifuge at 300 g for 5 mins and discard the supernatant.

  11. 3rd wash: Resuspend the cell pellet in 3 mL of PBS + 0.04% BSA.

  12. Filter the cells through a 40 µm cell strainer (FACS tubes) to remove clumps or debris.

  13. Aliquot 15 µL of cells + 15 µL of Trypan blue and count using Countess II.
    NOTE: Cells are kept on ICE during cell count!

    14. ** Thaw Gel beads from -80°C freezer for at least 30 mins, thaw 10x reagents, open PCR and open Chromium X


  14. Calculate and make aliquots of 1.2×106 cells/mL for each sample ( ~500 µL; depends on cell concentration).
    NOTE: If number of live cells > 1.2×106/mL, add cells and PBS+0.04% BSA following the table below.
